# NYC as a System

![skyline line drawing of New York City](blog/images/nyc-as-a-system.svg)

*By Kin Lane ([API Evangelist](https://apievangelist.com)) · July 15, 2026*

A system is what a system does. I am trying to understand what NYC does in this moment. After 15 years studying enterprise, institutional, and government systems from the inside-out and outside-in, I am learning that outside-in has its advantage in many circumstances. Even without access to systems, it enjoys reduced levels of politics, dogma, and baggage than doing it internally. We still need both, and nothing beats an honest top-down mandate from leadership that truly believes in change from any direction possible.

I am thinking about NYC as a system from the 500K level, rising above any single agency, while still working to align with the Mayor's and each agency's mission. I am taking my experience working in the federal government on APIs and open data, and looking for ways that I can transcend the business and politics of NYC using a simple, standards-based integration approach using APIs and MCP, orchestrated using Agent Skills that shape the data across agencies into processes that reflect what average New Yorkers would need.

NYC does have an active open data project at [opendata.cityofnewyork.us](https://opendata.cityofnewyork.us), but when you do a comparison with what is available as tables, forms, Excel files, and other formats across [67 NYC domains](https://nyc.apievangelist.com), there is a gap. Then when you gather all of the entities available across these domains, produce a crosswalk, and a complete set of APIs — completed with [MCP interfaces for AI applications](https://nyc.apievangelist.com/experience.html) — you begin to see where the low hanging fruit is when it comes to making sense of NYC as a system, and begin doing the work to align with the processes average New Yorkers care about.

There is a lot to learn in here. I am just getting started. I know enough to understand the political undertones that exist within each of the 67 domains, and why there are gaps in this data. And this is just the outputs of the system — this isn't an internal view of the system. Regardless, there is a lot that can be done from the outside-in. There are plenty of reasons why open data wasn't enough, and understanding what is needed to truly make a city programmable as a system. This NYC — Digital Modernization work is meant to try and understand how open data work fell short, and what is needed to make NYC programmable in desktop, web, mobile, and AI applications.
